About Rouzanna, daughter of Avetis, Petrosyan (Aleksanyan)

Rouzanna, daughter of Avetis, Petrosyan (Aleksanyan), was born on August 2, 1963, in Yerevan, Armenia.

She graduated from Secondary School No. 166 in Yerevan, after which she continued her education specializing in the field of radio engineering. In 1978–1979, at the invitation of her aunt, she took her first steps in the study of fashion design in the Czechoslovak Republic.

In 1981, she married in Yerevan and found happiness in raising her two children, Shoghakat and Avetis.

In 1990, the family moved to the United States, where they welcomed two more children, Meline and Hovhannes.

In 2006, Rouzanna completed the Criminal Justice program at Los Angeles Valley College, simultaneously finishing coursework in Child Development. Pursuing further education, she completed courses at Yale University and received her psychologist's license. She then enrolled at American Premier University, where she graduated with honors with Bachelor's and then Master's degrees in Business Administration. At the same university, she defended her doctoral thesis and earned a Ph.D. in Business Administration.

Rouzanna actively collaborates with and supports charitable organizations, providing assistance to families in need. She serves as the Vice President of the "Unity Club" of Los Angeles and, since 2020, has participated in various seminars across different countries.

She appears in numerous television programs and is the author of several articles and personal books, including Desert Flower, Black Swan's Dream, and Secret of the Eclipse. Rouzanna has also become a member of the Writers Union of the Republic of Armenia since 2025.

She is blessed and happy with her seven grandchildren.
